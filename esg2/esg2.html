OUR PHILOSOPHY

Suez recognises the importance of sustainable investing and is committed to promoting sound ESG management practices
within its portfolio. Thoughtful and well-implemented ESG management can enhance profitability and create significant value for
investors and stakeholders.

We have therefor developed a comprehensive policy on responsible investing that addresses identifcation, analysis and management
of potential ESG risks and opportunities, both before investment and post-investment. Addionally, the appropriate disclosure of all
ESG consideration is required by the underlying funds in which South Suez invests.

OUR ESG FUND RATING
We have integrated an ESG Management System into our bespoke Risk Operating System. This allows us to adopt a methodological
approach to manage environmental & social risks and impacts in a structured way on an ongoing basis.
 An ESG rating is assigned to all the funds within our portfolio. The scorecard is based on the following criteria:
1
PORTFOLIO CONSTITUENTS
Are the fund managers’ investments deemed risky from an ESG perspective?
2
LPA PROVISION
Do the fund formation documents contain provisions for ESG reporting, and has the fund manager complied with those provisions?
3
ECONOMIC SUSTAINABILITY
Are the funds’ portfolio companies performing well so that they can sustain employment and pay taxes?
4
RESPONSIBLE INVESTMENT PRINCIPLES
Does the fund subscribe to sustainability principles such as the UNPRI, IFC standards, etc.?
5
RESOURCES
Does the fund manager have sufficient resources (staff, ESG system) to execute its ESG policy?
6
CHARITY
Does the fund manager have a charitable programme, which makes donations regularly?

SIGNATORY OF UNPRI

The Principles for Responsible Investment reflects the increasing
relevance of environmental, social and corporate governance issues to
investment practices. South Suez will only invest with fund managers that
have ESG policies that are in line with the key standards set by the UNPRI.

In signing the Principles, we as investors publicly commit to adopt and
implement them, where consistent with our fiduciary responsibilities.
We also evaluate the effectiveness and improve the content of the Principles
over time. Aiming to improve our ability to meet commitments to
beneficiaries as well as better align our investment activities with the
broader interests of society.

IFC SUSTAINABILITY FRAMEWORK

South Suez requires fund managers to comply at a minimum with the legal
regulatory requirements in the jurisdictions in which they operate and to
work towards relevant international best practice standards. This includes
the IFC’s Sustainability Framework which has become a benchmark for
environmental and social risk management amongst our fund managers.

We use this framework to classify our portfolio companies into different
ESG risk categories; Category A being business activities with potential
significant adverse environmental or social risks and/or impacts that are
diverse, irreversible, or unprecedented. This category is closely
monitored through their ESG reports
